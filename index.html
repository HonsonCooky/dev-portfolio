Now, I've traded sheet music for syntax; my compositions resonate through elegant algorithms, harmonizing data structures and orchestrating efficient solutions. I'm prepared to bring my distinctive mix of creativity and expertise to exciting projects. Are you ready for me? Welcome to my melodic journey through bytes, nibbles and bits! Check out some of my opinions and thoughts in my /Blogs.

Neovim
Details Shortly after entering the industry, I switched to Neovim. During my time at university, proper finger placement wasn't a priority due to the sheer volume of learning materials. Upon entering the workforce, the high cadence of typing revealed the potential harm of improper finger placement. Learning proper finger placement then highlighted issues with staggered keyboard layouts. To solve this, I adopted a 40% ortholinear keyboard, which minimized wrist movement. However, this led to another problem: frequent hand movements to the mouse. I addressed this by integrating Vim motions in VSCode, which eventually led me to Neovim. I utilized Neovim as my only editor for a year before switching to Vim.
Vim
Details

After spending a year with Neovim, I found myself wanting to explore some more uncommon programming languages and frameworks - Blazor and F#. Neovim tooling around these technologies was lack-luster at the time, which meant I needed to: A) Adopt another IDE, B) Avoid learning the language/framework, or C) Build the tooling myself. Broadening my horizons, I questioned whether my reliance on language servers and syntax highlighting was truly the path I wanted to follow ( Reddit Post). After exploring the capabilities of pure, language-agnostic Neovim, I discovered that I actually only needed the based Vim functionality to explore this world. Whilst I haven't adopted this at work yet, I am using this LSP-less Vim configuration as my personal IDE setup at the moment.
